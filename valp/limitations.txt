# Volatility-Adaptive Lending Protocol (VALP) Limitations

This document outlines the explicit boundaries of the VALP architecture.
It acts as an objective reference for what the system is securely capable
of executing and where it falls short by design or by deliberate scope decision.

---

## What It Can Do

- **Single-Asset Markets**: Power isolated lending environments where users
  supply and borrow the exact same underlying asset (e.g. purely WETH pools).
- **Endogenous Risk Mitigation**: Automatically restrict LTV and Liquidation
  Thresholds as real-time market volatility scales, shielding lenders during
  turbulent events.
- **Dynamic Circuit Breaking**: Freeze all new borrowing the moment recorded
  volatility exceeds the extreme threshold (70%).  Oracle failures also trigger
  the freeze (fail-closed).
- **Continuous Compounding**: Accrue interest through a 3rd-order Taylor series
  approximation of e^(rt) — materially more accurate than simple or 2nd-order
  models at high rates and long accrual intervals.
- **Fail-Closed Oracle Policy**: When the volatility oracle is unavailable or
  its data is stale (> 1 hour), the system assumes maximum volatility (WAD).
  This tightens all safety parameters rather than silently relaxing them.
- **Two-Step Admin Safeguards**: Ownership transfers for both LendingPool and
  InterestRateModel require a pending-owner acceptance step, preventing
  accidental admin key loss.
- **Timelocked Reserve Factor**: Changes to the protocol fee rate are subject to
  a mandatory 2-day timelock, giving depositors time to exit before yield is
  redirected to reserves.
- **Collateral Top-Up**: Users with existing borrows can always deposit more
  collateral to improve their health factor — the standard mechanism used in
  Aave and Compound.
- **Third-Party Repayment**: Any address can repay debt on behalf of a borrower
  via `repayOnBehalf()`, enabling keeper-based health management without
  requiring privileged engine access.

---

## What It Cannot Do (By Design)

- **Multi-Asset / Cross-Collateral Lending**: The `LendingPool` contract
  supports only one asset token per deployment.  Users cannot deposit WBTC to
  borrow USDC within the same pool.  Multi-asset support requires an asset
  registry and per-asset oracle feeds.

- **Price-Based Liquidations**: There is no external price oracle. Liquidations
  trigger solely when the health factor (based on utilisation and interest
  accumulation) falls below 1.0, not because Token X crashed on an exchange.

- **Liquidation at 100% Utilisation**: If the pool is 100% utilised there are
  no idle tokens for a liquidator to receive.  Liquidation is structurally
  blocked until some repayment occurs.

- **EIP-4626 Compliance**: Pool shares do not conform to the EIP-4626 vault
  standard, breaking direct composability with aggregators like Yearn.

- **Flash Loans**: No uncollateralised flash-loan endpoints are provided.

---

## Known Remaining Risks (Scope Limitations)

- **Oracle Walk-Manipulation (Issue 6)**: With MIN_UPDATE_INTERVAL = 5 s and
  WINDOW_SIZE = 12, a whitelisted feeder can fill the ring buffer with biased
  prices in ~60 seconds.  Mitigation requires either TWAP-weighted averaging
  inside getVolatility() or a much larger window (e.g. 168 entries at 10-minute
  intervals).  This is flagged for a v2 redesign.

- **Virtual Share Seed (Issue 15)**: VIRTUAL_SHARES = 1,000 wei provides
  minimal inflation-attack resistance for 18-decimal tokens.  A production
  deployment should either seed with real tokens at construction (e.g.
  1,000e18) or follow EIP-4626's 10^(decimals/2) guidance, accepting a small
  bootstrapping deposit from the deployer.

- **globalBorrowIndex Overflow**: At MAX_RATE = 200% APR the index doubles
  every ~6 months.  The 365-day elapsed cap limits per-call growth, but a
  70-year-old pool at max rate could theoretically approach uint256 saturation.
  SafeMath or an index cap is advisable for multi-decade deployments.

- **Interest Index Approximation Error**: The 3rd-order Taylor series still
  underestimates e^(rt) by ~2% at 200% APR / 1-year window.  For maximal
  accuracy, implement a pre-computed table or Solidity fixed-point exponential.

- **Single-Asset Borrow Economics**: Borrowing the same asset you deposit is
  only economically meaningful for looping yield — which the circuit breaker
  partially inhibits.  Multi-asset support or explicit yield-loop documentation
  is recommended before a production launch.
